## Results

_Canonical results live in `results/REPORT.md` (regenerated by `make_report.py`).
Test set = noise-ceiling triplets (testset1). Snapshot below._

### Table 1 — Zero-shot vs aligned odd-one-out accuracy (test split)
| Model | Family | d | Zero-shot | Aligned | Δ | % ceiling |
|-------|--------|---|-----------|---------|---|-----------|
| SigLIP ViT-B/16 | Contrastive | 768 | 0.468 | **0.616** | +0.148 | 91.6% |
| ResNet-50 (sup.) | Supervised | 2048 | 0.433 | 0.613 | +0.180 | 91.1% |
| DINOv2 ViT-B/14 | SSL | 768 | 0.408 | 0.609 | **+0.200** | 90.5% |
| CLIP ViT-B/16 | Contrastive | 512 | **0.474** | 0.604 | +0.130 | 89.8% |
| ViT-B/16 (sup.) | Supervised | 768 | 0.436 | 0.593 | +0.157 | 88.0% |

Headline: all 5 converge to **0.59–0.62 (88–92% of the 0.673 ceiling)** regardless
of starting point; the biggest gains go to the weakest baselines (DINOv2 +0.20).

### Table 2 — Cross-model transfer (test acc; row = source W, col = target features; shared 256d)
| W \ target | CLIP | DINOv2 | ResNet | SigLIP | ViT |
|-----------|------|--------|--------|--------|-----|
| **CLIP** | 0.593 | 0.412 | 0.440 | 0.417 | 0.424 |
| **DINOv2** | 0.415 | 0.576 | 0.396 | 0.391 | 0.414 |
| **ResNet** | 0.448 | 0.393 | 0.566 | 0.402 | 0.396 |
| **SigLIP** | 0.431 | 0.398 | 0.393 | 0.604 | 0.389 |
| **ViT** | 0.466 | 0.390 | 0.413 | 0.409 | 0.551 |
| _baseline W=I_ | 0.390 | 0.361 | 0.353 | 0.375 | 0.379 |

Diagonal (own W) 0.55–0.60 ≫ off-diagonal 0.39–0.47. Off-diagonal beats W=I but
only recovers **~15–25%** of the within-model gain → alignment is mostly
**model-specific**, with a weak shared linear component (CLIP is the best donor).

### RSA vs human SPoSE (Spearman)
| | CLIP | DINOv2 | ResNet | SigLIP | ViT |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| baseline | 0.419 | 0.144 | 0.287 | 0.319 | 0.146 |
| aligned | 0.811 | 0.793 | 0.831 | 0.828 | 0.775 |

Raw RSA varies wildly (DINOv2/ViT most "rotated away"); after alignment all
converge to **0.78–0.83**.

### Hardest categories post-alignment (consistent across ALL models)
office supply, furniture, kitchen tool, musical instrument, medical equipment, toy
— abstract/functional categories resist linear alignment everywhere.

## Decisions & deviations
_Record any scope changes, hyperparameter choices, or assumptions here so results stay reproducible._
- **Two baselines exist.** `zeroshot.json` = cosine on *raw* features (true out-of-box). `train_transform` console "baseline" = cosine on *standardized* features (standardization is itself part of the affine transform, so it's already a bit higher). `REPORT.md` Table 1 reports raw zero-shot → aligned (the headline Δ). Don't mix them.
- Test set = noise-ceiling triplets (testset1, 15,640 rows); val = regular held-out triplets. Aligned val (~0.59) ≈ aligned test (~0.61), so no obvious test-set leakage artifact.
- CLIP yields its 512-d projected embedding on this transformers version; if it returns 768-d pooled on another version that's expected (see `_as_tensor`).
- Phase-3 W is square in native space; Phase-4 transfer uses PCA→256 shared space.
